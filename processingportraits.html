How Does a Portrait Look Like In Processing?

When making portraits using digital/new media softwares, Processing is one of the best tools you can use to make such kinds of art. By experimenting with coding, including shapes, colors, positions, and sizes, user can make scenery and images that could also be made with such physical materials as ink and paint. It is all about making sure the colors, sizes, shapes, and positions are just right. For my portraits, I wanted to make a day and night dynamic, with two versions of the same scene in day and night. When these images are split in half and those half from opposite images are put together, viewers can see a clear contrast between day and night, reminding us of how differences make the world so colorful and interesting.

Check out the code down below!
